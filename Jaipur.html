Top Places of Jaipur

  • Amber Fort

  • Amber Fort was built in the 16th century by Raja Man Singh,a rajput king, and was later expanded by his successors.

    The Fort is a blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles, with a mix of Hindu and Muslim influences

  • Birla mandir

  • Birla mandir is also known as Laxmi Narayan Temple, is a beautiful white marble temple located in jaipur,

    Rajasthan,India.The temple is built in the Nagra style of architecture.

  • City palace

  • City palace was built in the 18th century by Maharaja Sawai jai Singh ||,the founder of jaipur

    The palace is a blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture styles.

  • Hawa mahal

  • The Hawa Mahal is a five-storey building, and it is the tallest building in the world that has been built without a

    a foundation. It has a curved architecture that leans at an 87 degree angle, and a pyramidal shape which has helped it stay erect for centuries.

  • Jal mahal

  • Jal Mahal was constructed on artificial Man Sagar Lake. The lake was constructed by damming the Dravyavati river in 1610.

    The palace is surrounded by the Aravalli hills and was initially built in 1596 AD for special reasons. The famine in the 15th century was the reason for the construction of Jal Mahal.

  • Jantar mantar

  • Jantar Mantar is a testament of the architectural brilliance and astronomical innovations of the Rajput era.

    Built by the Rajput king Sawai Jai Singh II - the founder of Jaipur city, the monument was completed in 1734.
